    "Why does your website look like that?"

    Uploaded: Sun, 04 Aug 2025 13:54 CDT My site looks like it was made in the mid-oughts. But why?

    As I'm rebuilding this site, I feel its worth answering this question for myself and for those who are curious. I think it comes down to a few reasons: accessibility, ease of writing, and preference. I'll try my best to explain what I mean.

    Accessibility

    I'll preface this section by saying I'm not, by any metric, an expert in accessibility. It is important that we all be mindful of accessibility. Folks smarter than me have demonstrated time and again that accessibility, in addition to making systems remotely tolerable for some, benefits everybody.

    I'm working on a separate, better researched post about web accessibility. In the meantime, it suffices to say that concise, machine-readable HTML is the most feasible way for me to keep this site accessible.

    Ease of writing

    Secondly, I write pages like this by hand. I've used tools like jekyll, org-publish, and tex4ht in the past for writing blog posts, but I find that it's just too difficult or inconsistent for my purposes. I much prefer having control over the page's markup.

    Thus, for a similar reason to accessibility, keeping the HTML simple and semantic is one of the only feasible ways to do this. I don't want to hand-write fifty divs, or copy and paste templates to that effect.

    A nice benefit of hand-writing these pages is stability. It reminds me of when you visit an old BBC page and you're teleported back to the 2000's; as long as the filepaths stay the same, this page can sit unchanged.

    Preference

    Finally, I'm just a fan of the late 90's early 00's look. It harkens to a time when you didn't need a mega-corporation in order to make a page just about you. (I am hosting this on GitHub, but I've written a webserver before and want to use it in the future!)

    Nothing beats accessible, timeless simplicity, and I think that's pretty cool.
